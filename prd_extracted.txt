PathwayIQ
AI-Powered College Pathway Planner
1. Executive Summary
PathwayIQ is a web application that helps high school and community college students build a precise, personalized academic roadmap toward a bachelor's degree — accounting for their current grade level, AP/IB exam credits, chosen major, target university, and desired graduation timeline.
The product solves a painful and costly problem: students routinely waste semesters taking wrong courses, retaking classes unnecessarily, or failing to leverage credits they already have. PathwayIQ eliminates this with a Gemini AI-powered planner backed by a structured database of official credit policies, ASSIST.org articulation agreements, IGETC requirements, and 4-year university curriculum data.
1.1 The Core User Journey
A student answers a short set of questions (grade level, graduation goal, pathway type, AP/IB scores, target major + school). The app then produces an interactive, editable semester plan with every course they need to take — and when to take it — to hit their goal.
2. Problem Statement
2.1 The Problem
Navigating college planning is fragmented, confusing, and often requires expensive consultants or luck. Students face:
Unclear credit transfer policies between CCs and UCs/CSUs
No tool that integrates AP/IB credits + CC articulation + major requirements together
Course sequencing mistakes that add a semester or year to their degree
Wasted prerequisite chains — taking Calc I at CC when AP Calc BC already covers it
First-generation students especially lack access to this institutional knowledge
2.2 Market Gap
Existing tools are partial solutions only:
PathwayIQ is the first product to combine live database accuracy with AI planning intelligence into a single, student-facing tool.
3. Goals & Non-Goals
3.1 Goals
Allow students to input their academic profile and instantly receive a personalized degree pathway
Support UC system (all 9 undergraduate campuses), CSU system (all 23 campuses), and California Community Colleges
Handle AP, IB, CLEP, and dual enrollment credits with accurate per-school credit policies
Support both the CC→Transfer pathway (2+2 or accelerated) and direct 4-year university pathway
Respect HS enrollment constraints — cap course load when student is still in high school
Account for IGETC completion requirements for CC→UC/CSU transfer
Use Gemini AI to generate schedule logic and natural language explanations; use a structured database for all official credit rules
Provide a semester-by-semester, course-by-course visual plan the student can explore and optionally adjust
3.2 Non-Goals (v1.0)
Out-of-state or private universities (Harvard, MIT, etc.) — future version
Graduate school planning
Financial aid or scholarship planning
Real-time integration with registrar systems for live seat availability
Mobile native apps — responsive web only at launch
Account creation / persistence — stateless for v1.0; save via export

5. Feature Specifications
5.1 Student Profile Intake
A multi-step form collecting all inputs needed to build the plan. Designed to be completable in under 5 minutes.
Step 1 — Academic Background
Current grade level: 9th, 10th, 11th, 12th, or "1st year CC", "2nd year CC"
Enrollment status: Full-time high school, Dual enrollment, CC only, Gap year
Target graduation date (by semester: e.g. Spring 2028)
Step 2 — Credit Bank
AP exams: searchable dropdown of all 38 AP subjects; input score (3/4/5) or "score pending"
IB exams: HL/SL, subject, score (4–7) or "score pending"
CLEP exams: subject + score
Dual enrollment / concurrent CC courses: course name, school, grade
The system maps these to specific credit grants per target school using the database
Step 3 — Pathway Choice
Option A: Direct 4-year university (select UC or CSU campus, indicate if already admitted)
Option B: CC first then transfer (select CC + target UC/CSU for transfer)
Option C: CC only (associate's degree or transfer path without specifying 4-year yet)
Step 4 — Major + School
Select target university from supported list
Select major from that university's catalog (database-backed, searchable)
Option to select a "most likely" major if undecided, for planning purposes
Step 5 — Constraints & Preferences
Maximum units per semester (light / standard / heavy — or custom number)
Semesters to skip (e.g. "I'm not taking summer 2026")
Priority: Finish as fast as possible / Balanced load / Minimize cost (more CC time)
5.2 Hybrid AI + Database Planning Engine
This is the core of PathwayIQ. The engine has two distinct layers:
Layer 1 — Credit & Articulation Database
A structured database containing verified, citable policy data. This layer is NOT AI-generated — it is curated from official sources and updated regularly.
AP/IB credit tables for every UC and CSU campus
ASSIST.org articulation data: which CC courses satisfy which UC/CSU major requirements
IGETC course lists per CC
4-year major curriculum maps (required courses, electives, unit totals) per UC/CSU major
Prerequisite chains for all courses in supported majors
Layer 2 — Gemini AI Scheduling Engine
Given the student profile + resolved credit map from Layer 1, Gemini is prompted to:
Assign courses to specific semesters respecting prerequisite chains
Respect unit caps (stricter caps during HS enrollment semesters)
Balance course load across semesters
Flag semesters where IGETC must be completed before transfer
Generate a natural-language explanation of each semester plan
Identify the critical path — which course sequence is the bottleneck to graduation
Suggest alternates if a course isn't offered in a given semester
5.3 Pathway Output — The Plan View
The main deliverable: a visual, interactive semester-by-semester roadmap.
Plan Layout
Timeline view: each semester is a column or card showing course names, units, and type (major req / GE / elective)
Color coding: courses from AP credit (green), CC courses (blue), university courses (purple), completed (gray)
Unit counter per semester and running total
"Transfer point" marker clearly shown for CC→transfer pathways
Milestone badges: IGETC completed, major prep done, total units reached
Plan Interactivity
Click any course to see: course description, why it's here, which requirement it satisfies
"What if" toggle: remove an AP credit and watch the plan rebuild
Drag-and-drop course to a different semester — Gemini checks if prerequisites are violated and warns
"Lighten this semester" button — AI redistributes one course forward
Export to PDF or shareable link
5.4 Credit Resolution Summary
Before showing the full plan, PathwayIQ shows the student a credit resolution card:
Table of every AP/IB exam entered → what credit it grants at their specific target school
Total units waived, GE categories covered, specific courses skipped
Warnings for credits that require a minimum score the student may not meet
Clear note if any credits are "pending score" and how the plan changes if they don't receive credit
5.5 IGETC Tracker (CC→Transfer Path)
Visual checklist of all 7 IGETC areas
Shows which are satisfied by AP credits, which by planned CC courses
Warns if student is on track to transfer without completing IGETC (delays transfer acceptance)

Prompt Architecture
Each planning request sends Gemini a structured system prompt + a JSON context payload containing:
Student profile (grade, constraints, semester availability)
Resolved credit map (which courses/GEs are already satisfied)
Major requirement list with prerequisite graph (from database)
IGETC completion status (for transfer paths)
Optimization preference (fast / balanced / cost-efficient)
Gemini returns a structured JSON response:
semesters[]: array of semester objects, each with courses[], units, notes
critical_path[]: the prerequisite chain determining earliest possible graduation
warnings[]: any constraint violations, pending-score risks, or gaps
summary: natural language explanation of the full plan

7. Data Strategy
7.1 Data Sources
AP Credit Policies: Each UC and CSU campus publishes official AP credit tables (PDF or web). Scraped + manually verified annually.
IB Credit Policies: Same approach as AP; published per campus.
CC→UC/CSU Articulation: ASSIST.org provides downloadable articulation agreements. ASSIST also offers a public API for some data.
IGETC Course Lists: Published by each CC in their catalog. Requires per-CC scraping.
Major Curriculum Maps: Available from each university's department websites. Most detailed and most labor-intensive to capture.
Course Prerequisites: Embedded in university course catalogs (often PDF or searchable web catalogs).
7.2 Data Update Cadence
Full refresh: Once per academic year (July–August, before fall enrollment)
Spot updates: Triggered by user-reported discrepancies or known policy changes
Version tagging: Every plan generated is tagged with the data version used, so old plans can flag if policies have changed
7.3 Launch Data Scope
v1.0 will launch with full data coverage for:
All 9 UC undergraduate campuses (AP/IB credits + major curricula for top 20 majors per campus)
All 23 CSU campuses (AP/IB credits; major curricula for top 10 majors per campus)
Top 30 California Community Colleges by transfer volume to UC/CSU (ASSIST articulation + IGETC)
8. User Experience Flow
8.1 Full Flow
Landing page → CTA: "Build My Plan"
Intake wizard: 5 steps (profile, credits, pathway, major/school, constraints) — progress bar shown
"Calculating your pathway..." loading screen (Gemini call happens here, 3–8 seconds)
Credit Resolution Summary screen — confirm what AP/IB credits apply
Full Pathway Plan view — interactive semester timeline
User can adjust inputs, re-run plan, export PDF, or share link
8.2 HS Student Edge Case Handling
When the student indicates they are currently enrolled in high school:
Any semesters while still in HS are flagged as "HS Active" semesters
The AI enforces a strict unit cap for these semesters (default: max 2 college courses / 6 units concurrent with HS)
The system surfaces a note: "We've limited your college course load for Spring 2026 since you'll still be in high school"
Summer terms after senior year are treated as post-HS and allow standard loads
8.3 Score Pending Handling
AP/IB scores marked "pending" are planned optimistically (credit assumed)
A banner on the plan reads: "This plan assumes you receive credit for [X] exams. If scores don't meet the threshold, [Y] courses may be added."
A toggle lets the student view the plan "with credit" vs. "without credit" for any pending exam
